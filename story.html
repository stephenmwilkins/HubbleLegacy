Origins

Lyman Spitzer

The idea of a space observatory was first proposed by the American astronomer Lyman Spitzer shortly after the Second World War.

SAZERAC works to support the high-redshift / epoch of reionisation community through the organisation of annual virtual (northern hemisphere) summer meetings, regular specialist sessions (SIPS), and the maintenance of a community mailing list. Virtually all of the talks were recorded and are available via our our YouTube channel. To contact us please message us at hello@sazerac-conference.org.
